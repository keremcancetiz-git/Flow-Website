The Hardware Loop

An IR source emits a broadband beam. The moving mirror creates an interference pattern (interferogram). The gas cell absorbs specific wavelengths based on the molecule's chemical bonds. A Fourier Transform converts the detector's raw signal into the readable spectrum above.

Pressure Broadening

As pressure increases, molecules collide more frequently. These collisions perturb the energy states of the molecules during absorption, which "spreads out" (broadens) the spectral peaks over a larger range of wavenumbers (Lorentzian profile).

Why Stability is Critical

Engineers use the area under the peak, or peak height, to calculate concentration ($c$) using the Beer-Lambert law ($A=\varepsilon l c$). If your reactor's exhaust temperature or pressure fluctuates wildly, the peak shapes distort and density drops, destroying your calibration curves and resulting in false readings.

Cross-Section (σ)

Mechanically, think of this as the "hitbox" of the molecule. In physics, it represents the probability of a photon inducing a transition to a higher vibrational energy state.

Vibration & Heat

When a "hit" occurs, the photon vanishes, and its energy is transferred to the molecule. The molecule vibrates faster (excitation). In a real detector, we measure the absence of these photons.

The Engineering Reality

Unstable pressure causes collisions that "reset" the vibration phase early (collisional broadening). This is the microscopic reason why your spectral peaks widen and calibrations fail.
